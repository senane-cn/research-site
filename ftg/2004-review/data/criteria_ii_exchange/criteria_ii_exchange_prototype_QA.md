# Criterion ii Exchange Prototype QA

This is a provisional circular-network prototype built from WHC Criterion (ii) OUV coding. It is not a final reviewed network and is not an official UNESCO/ICOMOS dataset.

- Original OUV edge count: 75
- Prototype coded edge count after patches: 75
- Edges marked as coded_needs_review: 4
- Provisional review-derived edges added: 37
- Final combined prototype edge count: 112
- Nodes used: 31
- Remaining unclear / caution rows: 6

## Removed / Downgraded / Re-coded Edges

- City of Quito | Southern Europe -> Andean America | Criterion ii statement describes Quito school influence within the Audiencia; colonial fusion may be valid but needs review.
- City of Potosí | South Asia -> Andean America | Indian influences means indigenous/local in the Potosi criterion ii statement, not South Asia.
- Holašovice Historic Village | Southern Europe -> Central Europe | South Bohemian Folk Baroque is likely an internal Central European vernacular/baroque fusion.
- Portuguese City of Mazagan (El Jadida) | South Asia -> North Africa | Route to India does not mean India influenced Mazagan.
- Mines of Rammelsberg, Historic Town of Goslar and Upper Harz Water Management System | Central Europe -> Transregional / Multiple | Rammelsberg recoded as mining and water-management technical influence.
- Chartres Cathedral | Southern Europe -> Western Europe | Chartres recoded as Gothic architectural influence.
- Chartres Cathedral | Central Europe -> Western Europe | Chartres recoded as Gothic architectural influence.

## Known Limitations

- Provisional review-derived edges are interpretive seed edges for visualization testing and remain excluded from any final network claim.
- `Transregional / Multiple` is a schematic node for global, maritime, route, pilgrimage, or multi-party exchanges where a bilateral pair would be misleading.
- Several criterion ii statements identify influence or exchange but not a precise source-target pair; these are retained in the unclear table or marked `needs_later_review`.
- The circular layout shows relationship structure, not geographic distance or chronology.
