# Phase 2B.8b Final Method Note

This dataset is a probable reconstruction of FTG Typological Framework assignments. It is not original ICOMOS working data.

## T06 Vernacular Architecture

T06_VERNACULAR is not treated as a lower-status or rural-only category. It denotes locally or regionally established building types, traditional construction systems, craft knowledge, local materials, and culturally embedded building traditions. Some vernacular cases may be urban, monumental, technically sophisticated, or high-status within their own cultural context. Classification must avoid judging non-Western earthen, timber, stone, or indigenous architecture as “vernacular” merely because it differs from Western classical or academic architectural traditions.

Examples:
- Old Walled City of Shibam: possible vernacular urbanism / earthen high-rise urban tradition, not “primitive” architecture.
- Kizhi Pogost / wooden church traditions: possible monumental vernacular craft tradition.
- Old Town of Quedlinburg: timber-framed urban/town architecture, where T04 and T06 may overlap.
- Chaco Culture: do not automatically treat indigenous monumental / settlement architecture as T06 unless traditional building type or construction system is central.
- Koutammakou: strong local architectural and settlement tradition, appropriate T06 main.

## T04 Cautious Downgrade Rule

Downgrade T04 only where T04 is clearly generic, incidental, or added to archaeological sites without independent architectural / ensemble value. Do not automatically downgrade T04 in historic cities, medinas, religious ensembles, or palace/monument complexes if architectural/ensemble value remains central.

## T07 Cautious Downgrade Rule

Downgrade T07 only where religious evidence is incidental or limited to components within an archaeological site or historic city. Retain T07 main where religious property identity is central: sacred site, monastery, temple/church/mosque complex, pilgrimage centre, religious institution, or religious landscape.

## Diagnostics

FTG Table 5 remains a calibration and diagnostic reference only. Phase 2B.8b applies explicit user decisions and Koutammakou verification; it does not alter labels to fit aggregate counts.
